Act 4 — Whole Room

The Confrontation

You've built something. Now hold it up to the light. This act is about looking honestly at what your redesign assumed — and where the approaches you use every day both support and limit the students in front of you.

/// How we spend 25 minutes
5 min — Presenter introduces the tension
2 min — The key contrast (structured teaching / UDL)
5 min — Table discussion: student voice question
8 min — Table audit of your Act 3 redesign
5 min — Whole-room share: one finding per table

Structured Teaching

What structured teaching offers

Clear, predictable structure that reduces anxiety
Visual supports that remove language as a barrier
Defined tasks with a clear start, steps, and finish
Reduced cognitive load — students know what is expected
Routine as a scaffold for independence

Universal Design for Learning

What UDL asks us to add

Multiple ways to enter the learning, not just one
Content that can be accessed in more than one format
Flexibility at the edges of a clear structure
Student agency — some choice in how they engage
Multiple ways to show understanding, not just one

/// The position

"Structured teaching honours what you know.
UDL honours what you don't."

These are not opposites. UDL does not ask you to remove structure. It asks you to design structure so that more students can enter it.

/// Table Discussion — 5 min

Table discussion

How should we incorporate student voice into the co-design of our lessons?

What would it look like to involve students in deciding how they access content?

What would it look like to involve students in deciding how they show what they know?

What gets in the way of doing that in your context?

/// Table Audit — 8 min

Look at what you built in Act 3

Hold your redesign up against these questions — then share one finding with the room

Where did you keep the structure? Was that the right call — and for which students?

Where did you change or remove something? What did you assume about your students when you made that decision?

Which of the four students from Act 2 is best served by your redesign — and which is still not reached?

Is there anything in your redesign that would create a new barrier for a student you weren't thinking about?

Where does your own teaching preference show up in what you built?
