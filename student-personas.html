Act 2 — The Diagnosis

The Students

Your table will work with one of these students through the three rounds of Act 2. Read the profile — then find the moment the lesson breaks for them.

Malcolm

Age 6

AAC / PECS user 1:1 support Fine motor challenges Responds to photographs

Communicates using AAC and PECS. Responds well to photographs of real objects and familiar animals. Fine motor tasks — like handling small cards or writing — are difficult without significant support.

Sally

Age 9

Age-level reader Tactile learner Anxiety: unclear tasks Visual schedule user

Works independently when expectations are clear and a visual schedule is in place. Prefers hands-on, tactile experiences. Becomes anxious when task demands are ambiguous.

Priya

Age 13

Significant vision impairment Auditory learner Strong science interest Difficulty expressing

Prefers to receive information through listening. Has a strong interest in science, particularly space. Often finds it difficult to demonstrate or share what she has understood.

Lachlan

Age 15

Co-occurring ADHD Needs relevance Real-world application Disengages quickly

Needs to understand why a task matters before investing. Disengages when learning feels arbitrary or disconnected from real-world purpose. Works best when there is a clear, meaningful application.
