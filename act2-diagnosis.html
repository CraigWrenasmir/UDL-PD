Act 2 — Table Teams

The Analysis

Your task is to diagnose — not to fix. Look at the lesson from Act 1 through the lens of the student assigned to your table. Before you redesign anything, the room needs to build a shared understanding of what the lesson actually offers — and what it doesn't.

Your task

What does this lesson offer your student — and what does it not offer? Why?

Three rounds. Each one moves from specific to structural. You'll have 10 minutes per round, then a share-back at the end.

/// The Students

Malcolm

Age 6

AAC / PECS user 1:1 support Fine motor challenges Responds to photographs

Malcolm communicates using AAC and PECS. He responds well to photographs of real objects and familiar animals. Fine motor tasks — like handling small cards or writing — are difficult without significant support.

Sally

Age 9

Reads at age level Tactile learner Anxiety around unclear tasks Visual schedule user

Sally reads at age level and can work independently for short periods when expectations are clear and a visual schedule is in place. She prefers hands-on, tactile learning experiences and becomes anxious when task demands are ambiguous.

Priya

Age 13

Significant vision impairment Auditory learner Strong science interest Difficulty expressing learning

Priya has a significant vision impairment and prefers to receive information through listening. She has a strong interest in science — particularly space. She often finds it difficult to demonstrate or share what she has understood.

Lachlan

Age 15

Co-occurring ADHD Needs relevance Real-world application Disengages quickly

Lachlan needs to understand why a task matters before he can invest in it. He disengages quickly when learning feels arbitrary or disconnected from real-world purpose. Works best when there is a clear, meaningful application.

/// Three Rounds — 10 min each

Round 1 — The Stuck Moment

10 minutes

Where in this lesson does your student get stuck — and what exactly causes it?

Example: "Malcolm would get stuck at the card-sorting step — not because he doesn't understand living vs. non-living, but because fine motor handling of small cards is a barrier. The lesson tests his hands, not his understanding."

Round 2 — The Assumption Hunt

10 minutes

What does the lesson assume about this student — and is that assumption right?

Template: "This lesson assumes _________ can _________. But for _________, that assumption breaks because _________."

Round 3 — The Pattern

10 minutes

Is the assumption your table found specific to this student — or does it reveal something structural in the lesson design?

Template: "The lesson assumes all students can _________. That's not a gap for one student — it's a design flaw."

Share-back

Each table shares back to the room — 60 seconds per table. Your most significant finding from Round 3: the structural assumption you found. The room builds collective intelligence together.

Padlet

Add your table's key finding to the shared Padlet — scan the QR code on screen. This becomes the collective knowledge the room takes into Act 3.
