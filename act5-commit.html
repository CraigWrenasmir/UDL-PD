Act 5 — Individual → Room

Commit + Close

Three rounds. Each one builds on the last. Start individually — then bring it to your table. You came in with a lesson. You leave with a question.

/// How we spend 30 minutes
Round 1 — Reflect: 3 min individual + 5 min table
Round 2 — The Question: 3 min individual + 5 min table
Round 3 — Apply It: 8 min individual
Whole-room share: 4 min
Round 1 — Reflect 3 min individual → 5 min table

One thing today has made you question about the way you currently plan or support learning.

Think about a bias you may have about how you design or support lessons — and what you'd do differently because of it.

Share at your table. What did others notice that you missed?

Round 2 — The Question 3 min individual → 5 min table

Distil your reflection into one question you'll ask yourself before every lesson plan.

Not a checklist item — a genuine question you don't already know the answer to.

Share your question with your table. They'll help you sharpen it.

A good question names a tension. A great question makes you uncomfortable every time you ask it.

Round 3 — Apply It 8 min individual

Take a real lesson or session you're planning next week.

Ask your question. What changes?

Write one thing you'd do differently as a result.

Something specific enough to act on. Not a principle — a change.

Whole-room share

4 minutes. One question per table — the one you'll actually use. The question that stayed with you.
