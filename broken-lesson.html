Act 1 Artefact

This is The Broken Lesson — the shared starting point for the session. It's not a bad lesson made by a bad teacher. It's a recognisable, structured, well-intentioned task that carries the design assumptions we're here to examine. Read it as you would if you'd just picked it up in a staffroom. Then we'll look at it together.

Living and Non-Living Things

Name
Class
Date

Learning Intentions

I am learning to identify the characteristics of living things and distinguish them from non-living things.
I will know I can do this when I can list three characteristics of living things and correctly sort examples into each category.

Read This Passage

Living things are organisms that are alive. All living things share several important characteristics that set them apart from non-living things.

First, living things grow and develop over time. A seed grows into a plant. A baby grows into an adult. Second, living things need food and water to survive and to get energy. Third, living things can reproduce — this means they are able to make more of themselves. A dog can have puppies. A flower can produce seeds. Fourth, living things respond to changes in their environment. A plant grows towards light. An animal runs away from danger.

Non-living things do not have these characteristics. A rock is non-living because it does not grow, eat, or reproduce. A car is also non-living. Even though it moves and uses fuel, it was not born and cannot make more cars by itself.

Your Task

Step 1 — Read the passage above carefully.
Step 2 — Answer all four questions below in full sentences.
Step 3 — Check your work when you are finished.
Step 4 — Tick the FINISHED box at the bottom of the page.

1

Write three characteristics of living things in your own words.

2

Is a car living or non-living? Write your answer and explain why using evidence from the passage.

3

Give two examples of living things and two examples of non-living things that are not mentioned in the passage.

4

In your own words, explain what it means for a living thing to reproduce.

Finished — show your teacher

Act 1 Activity Using the UDL Bias Scanner on this lesson

Read through the lesson above. Then use the Bias Scanner — individually or with a partner — to examine what assumptions are built into its design. You don't need to fix anything yet. Just notice what you find.

As you work through it, consider:

Who is this lesson designed for? Who does it assume will be in the room?
What does a student need to already be able to do in order to access this lesson?
How many ways are there to enter this task? To show understanding?
Which of your students could complete this lesson as written — and which couldn't?
