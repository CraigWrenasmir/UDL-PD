Session Architecture: Five-Act Structure

This is your reference for the day. Each act has a different mode and cognitive demand — moving from individual reflection, to collaborative analysis, to fast-paced redesign, to whole-room discussion, to personal commitment. The relationship between structured teaching and universal design is a thread running through everything. You don't need to resolve it — you need to hold it. All tools below are optional — use what's useful, leave what isn't.

Preface

Before Act 1

Shared Language

10:00 – 10:15 15 minutes Presenter-led

Content

Structured Teaching — what it is, where it comes from, the four elements (physical structure, schedules, work systems, task organisation)
Universal Design for Learning — the architecture analogy, three principles (Engagement / Representation / Expression), the key mindset shift
How they relate — what they share, where they diverge, why the tension is productive
Close with the synthesis statement — then move into Act 1 without resolving it.
Act 1

Opening

The Mirror  → The Lesson

10:00 – 10:30 30 minutes Individual → Whole Room

Activities

A shared lesson is introduced — Living and Non-Living Things. Read it. Nothing else yet.
Step 1: "How would you design this lesson for your students?" — table discussion, 5 min. Real practice, not the ideal.
Step 2: "Who does this design serve well?" — which students in your class would thrive in what you just described? 2 min.
Step 3: "Who might find this harder?" — who is at the edges of what it reaches? 3 min.
Step 4: Whole-room harvest — one observation per table. 5 min.
Act 2

Table Teams

The Diagnosis

10:30 – 11:10 40 minutes Table Teams

Three Rounds

Round 1 — The Stuck Moment (10 min)

Your table takes one or two of the four students. Find the single moment in the lesson where that student gets stuck — a precise moment, a precise reason.

"Malcolm gets stuck here, because the lesson assumes he can..."

Round 2 — The Assumption Hunt (10 min)

Flip it. What assumption did the lesson designer make that caused the stuck moment? The critique stays on the design, not the student.

"This lesson was designed for a student who already knows how to..."

Round 3 — The Pattern (10 min)

Step back. What do the assumptions across all four students have in common? Is there one structural assumption causing multiple students to get stuck?

"Every student at our table gets stuck at the same moment — because the lesson assumes..."
Share-back (10 min) — one pattern per table. The room builds a collective picture of what this lesson was designed for, and who it was designed by.
Break 20 minutes — 11:10 to 11:30
Act 3

Hackathon with Constraints

The Rebuild

11:30 – 12:20 50 minutes Hackathon

Three Rounds

Round 1 — Engagement (15 min)

Redesign how students enter the lesson — what draws them in, gives them a reason to start, and lowers the initial barrier for all four students.

Constraint: "Every student must have a way in."
2-minute steal → grab one idea from the table next to you

Round 2 — Representation (15 min)

Redesign how students access the content. What else could carry the concept when the current format doesn't work?

Constraint: "The content must reach every student."
2-minute steal → grab one idea from the table next to you

Round 3 — Expression (15 min)

Redesign how students show what they know. For each student, what does genuine evidence of understanding look like when the mode of demonstration is no longer the obstacle?

Constraint: "Every student must be able to show you what they know."
Act 4

Whole Room

The Confrontation

12:20 – 12:45 25 minutes Whole Room

Activities

5 min — Presenter-led: The Structured Teaching / UDL tension introduced explicitly — after you've built something, not before.
"Structured teaching honours what you know. UDL honours what you don't."
5 min — Table discussion: "How should we incorporate student voice into the co-design of our lessons?"
8 min — Table reflection: Hold your redesign up against five audit questions. What assumptions did you bake in without noticing?
5 min — Whole-room share: One observation from the audit per table.
Act 5

Individual → Room

Commit + Close

1:00 – 1:30 30 minutes (incl. buffer) Individual → Room

Activities

Round 1 — Reflect (8 min)

Individual (3 min) then table (5 min). Two prompts:

"One thing today has made you question about the way you currently plan or support learning."

"And one thing you'll do differently because of it."

Round 2 — The Question (8 min)

Individual (3 min) then table (5 min). Distil your reflection into one question you'll ask yourself before every lesson plan. Your table helps you sharpen it.

Round 3 — Apply It (8 min)

Individual. Use your question on a real lesson or session you're planning next week. What changes?

Whole-room share (4 min) — one question per table. The one you'll actually use.
