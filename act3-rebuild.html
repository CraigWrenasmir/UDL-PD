Act 3 — Hackathon

The Rebuild

Three rounds. Each one redesigns a different dimension of the lesson. Each round has a constraint — read it before you start and keep it in mind as you build. Between rounds there's a two-minute steal: look at what the table next to you has done, and take one idea.

1

UDL Principle 1

Engagement

15 minutes
Constraint Every student must have a way in.

Redesign how students enter this lesson. Think about what draws them in, gives them a reason to start, and lowers the initial barrier — for all four students from Act 2. The predictable structure doesn't go away. Your job is to flex it at the edges, not remove it.

By the end

Your table has at least two different ways a student could enter this lesson — both of which preserve a clear, predictable structure.

UDL Principle 2

Representation

15 minutes
Constraint The content must reach every student.

Redesign how students access the content of this lesson. Think about every way the concept of living and non-living things could reach a learner — consider Malcolm, Sally, Priya and Lachlan specifically. What else could carry this concept beyond a visual card? Sound? Movement? Real objects? A conversation?

By the end

Your table has at least two additional ways the content could be accessed.

→

2-Minute Steal

2 min
3

UDL Principle 3

Expression

15 minutes
Constraint Every student must be able to show you what they know.

Redesign how students show what they know. Think about what demonstrating understanding of living and non-living things actually requires — versus what we assume it requires. For each of the four students, what would real evidence of understanding look like?

By the end

Your table has at least one way each of the four students could show genuine understanding — without the format getting in the way of the learning.
